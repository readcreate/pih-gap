Understanding Gap Analysis

Before we collect data, let's align on the conceptual framework. Click through each concept to explore.

Landscape Analysis

What Exists?

A broad survey of current programs, policies, actors, and resources. It asks: "Who is doing what, where?"


Focus: Descriptive inventory of existing MCH services, funding streams, and stakeholders.

Gap Analysis

What's Missing?

Compares what exists to what is needed. It asks: "Where are the critical unmet needs?"


Focus: Prescriptive. Identifies deficits in quality, coverage, or capacity to set actionable priorities.

Why this matters for PIH: A landscape analysis tells us there are 12 health facilities in a catchment area. A gap analysis tells us that 9 lack skilled birth attendants — and this gap drives 60% of preventable newborn deaths.

From Data → Decision → Accountability

Evidence-informed decision-making means systematically gathering the best available data — quantitative, qualitative, and community knowledge — to set priorities that are defensible, transparent, and trackable.

Not all gaps are equal. The Priority Matrix scores gaps on two axes: Severity (how critical?) and Feasibility (can we act now?).

⬇ LOW Feasibility

🟠 PLAN

High severity, harder to address — needs strategic planning

🟢 QUICK WIN

Example: EmOC is critically understaffed (high severity) AND the Ministry has a nurse redeployment program available (high feasibility) → Act Now.

Planning the Gap Analysis

This conference is the beginning. Now let's think together about how the full gap analysis should happen later this year.

Your input shapes the methodology

The gap analysis will only succeed if it reflects the realities of each site. Share your honest thinking on the questions below — there are no wrong answers. Your responses will inform how PIH structures and conducts the full gap analysis process.

🗓️
Timing & Cadence
When and how often should the gap analysis happen?

"What would be the ideal timing for conducting the full gap analysis at your site? What frequency makes sense — annual, biannual, other?"

👥
Ownership & Roles
Who should lead and participate?

"Who at your site should own the gap analysis process? Who needs to be at the table — and who is often left out?"

📊
Data Sources
What data should feed the analysis?

"What data sources exist at your site that should inform the gap analysis? What data is missing that you wish you had?"

🚧
Barriers

"What are the biggest barriers to conducting a meaningful gap analysis at your site?"

✅
